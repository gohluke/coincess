### What Went Wrong

1. **Trading the wrong coins.** The auto-discovery algorithm explicitly skipped BTC and ETH (line 897: `if (["USDC","USDT","BTC","ETH"].includes(coin)) continue`) and hunted for coins with 7+ bps spreads. Those spreads are wide because the coins are volatile, not because there's free money.

2. **No actual rebate.** At Tier 0 on Hyperliquid, maker fee is +1.5 bps (you pay). Rebates only kick in at very high volume tiers. Round-trip cost = ~3 bps minimum. The name "Rebate Farmer" was aspirational, not descriptive.

3. **Stop-loss fired on nearly every trade.** The 8 bps stop-loss triggered within seconds on volatile coins. Average losing trade: -$0.40. Average winning trade: +$0.15. The math never works with that ratio and a 15% win rate.

4. **Massive fee drag.** $16.55 in fees on $72K volume. The bot was a fee generation machine for Hyperliquid, not a profit machine for us.

5. **158 fills on GRIFFAIN alone.** The bot kept re-entering a coin it was consistently losing on because the "win bonus" scoring didn't penalize it fast enough.

## Lessons

1. **Kill switches should be more aggressive.** The `maxDailyLossUsd: 20` setting existed but the bot blew past it. Need a hard circuit breaker that actually halts trading.

2. **Never auto-discover coins for spread capture.** Spread capture only works on highly liquid, mean-reverting markets. A whitelist of proven coins is strictly better than auto-discovery.

3. **The strategy name was a lie.** There's no rebate at Tier 0. This was pure spread capture on micro-caps, which is a losing proposition. Either trade volume to reach rebate tiers (on majors), or don't call it rebate farming.

4. **Manual trades outperformed 792 bot fills.** 5 manual fills on BRENTOIL made +$3.39. 787 bot fills across 31 coins lost -$33.87. Discretion > algorithm when the algorithm is broken.

5. **Bot is now fully stopped.** No automation running. Future trading is manual only until a proven, backtested strategy is ready.
