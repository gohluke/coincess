## What Went Wrong

### 1. Panic sold a winning position
The "No $105" bet was the correct thesis. Oil at $101 with hours left in March was never going to hit $105. The shares would have settled at $1.00, returning ~$455 profit. Instead I sold at $0.325, locking in a $513 loss.

### 2. Misread the countdown timer
Both the Polymarket site and Based.app showed a countdown that made it look like the event was ending a day earlier than it actually was. This created false urgency and triggered the panic sell. The event actually resolved at end of day March 31, not March 30. I should have verified the actual resolution date independently.

### 3. Flipped to the opposite side on tilt
After selling the "No" position, I immediately bought "Yes" on $110 — a degenerate bet that oil would rally 10% in a day. This was emotional, not rational. The $0.17 price felt "cheap" but it was cheap because the probability was near zero.

### 4. Doubled down on the wrong direction
Buying "Yes $105" at $0.297 was adding to a losing thesis. I was now betting oil would spike, the exact opposite of what I believed 12 hours earlier.

### 5. Closed the hedge too early
The BRENTOIL long was meant to be held to $105 as insurance. Closing at $101.40 for $45 profit defeated the purpose. Had I held the hedge and the original "No" bet, the combined position would have been profitable regardless of outcome.

### 6. No plan for the plan
The original trade had a clear thesis and a hedge. But there was no written plan for when to exit, when to flip, or what would constitute a "changed thesis." Without written rules, emotions took over.

## Lessons

1. **If the thesis hasn't changed, don't change the trade.** Oil at $101 was never going to hit $105. Nothing changed — I just got scared.

2. **Never trust a UI countdown for resolution timing.** Always verify the actual event end date from the contract or rules, not a countdown timer on a frontend.

3. **Write the plan before entering.** Entry, target, stop, and the conditions under which you'd flip. If it's not written down, you'll rationalize anything in the moment.

4. **Hedges only work if you hold them.** The BRENTOIL long was a good idea but closing at $101.40 for pocket change defeated its purpose.

5. **Tilt is the most expensive emotion in trading.** The flip from "No" to "Yes" was pure tilt. Recognizing tilt and stepping away for 30 minutes would have saved $1,000.

6. **Small wins don't fix big mistakes.** The +$45 on Hyperliquid felt good in the moment but it was irrelevant against the -$1,010 Polymarket loss. Don't let a scalp distract from the real position.
